Who am I?

On paper, I am an economist from the University of Copenhagen. In life, I am much more than that.
From the outside, my upbringing seemed pretty normal and suburban, but from the inside of my mind, something was off. For a long time, I walked around like an empty shell, out of touch with myself, simply trying to keep up with the demands and expectations of my surroundings, until one day, when I was compelled to break free. I moved to New York with absolutely no plan in mind and lived there on/off for four years.

New York had space for me to explore and grow. It was during my time in New York that my interest in art and creativity blossomed. I met some wonderful, inspiring artists and took courses in art history at a university in the city. I was accepted into Sotheby’s and Christie’s art business programs, but unfortunately, due to my mental state, I realized that I had to go back home to Denmark to get help. That was three years ago, and now, I am here, happily working with clay. It has become my mission to share joy through the use of form and color, and bring it into people’s everyday lives.

Clay is my medium of choice, but my work is more than the physical outcome itself. #byValentin is all about using forms and colors to create a sense of delight and contentment – experiences beyond the physical. ​
